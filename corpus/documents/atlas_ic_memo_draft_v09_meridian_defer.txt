EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This preliminary IC draft recommended deferring approval of the Meridian acquisition until the deal team resolved three open items: customer concentration, the CTO departure, and a legal diligence question around revenue recognition. At this stage, the draft proposed pausing final negotiations rather than approving a signing mandate.

RATIONALE FOR DEFER RECOMMENDATION

1. Customer concentration had not yet been reconciled against management's ARR schedule. The draft assumed top-3 concentration could exceed 60% and requested confirmation from Customer 3 before signing.

2. Engineering diligence had not completed reference checks on the CTO departure. The draft treated the departure as a product-continuity risk until the retention plan for senior engineers was costed.

3. Legal diligence had not completed its assessment of the pending regulatory inquiry into implementation-fee revenue recognition.

SUPERSESSION NOTE

The final IC memo (doc_010) superseded this draft after the deal team reduced entry EV to $340M, added customer-renewal protections, modeled engineering retention packages, and added legal indemnity language.
