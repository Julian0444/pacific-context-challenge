ATLAS CAPITAL PARTNERS — INTERNAL MEMORANDUM
PROJECT CLEARWATER: INITIAL DUE DILIGENCE SUMMARY
March 15, 2024 | CONFIDENTIAL — VP AND ABOVE

TO:   VP Deal Team, Partners
FROM: Sarah Chen, Associate; Marcus Webb, VP

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Following initial management meetings and document review, this memo summarizes our Phase 1
diligence findings on Meridian Technologies ("the Company") under the internal project name
Clearwater. We recommend advancing to Phase 2 financial diligence.

BUSINESS QUALITY

ClearFlow is demonstrably sticky. Three of five reference customers contacted cited workflow
dependency and implementation cost as barriers to switching. Implementation cycles average 4–6
months, creating meaningful switching costs. The platform's core value proposition — automated
reconciliation exception handling — addresses a real operational pain point for mid-market regional
banks where manual processes are still prevalent.

GROWTH OBSERVATIONS

ARR growth of 34% in FY2023 is consistent with our internal thesis. However, new logo growth
slowed materially in H2 2023: the Company added only 4 new enterprise clients in Q4, compared
to 9 in the first three quarters combined. Management attributes this to sales team capacity
constraints and pipeline timing. We are reserving judgment pending access to the CRM.

KEY RISKS IDENTIFIED

  1. Customer Concentration: Top 3 customers represent ~58% of ARR. Loss of any single top
     customer would materially impact revenue in the near term.
  2. Sales Velocity: H2 2023 new logo slowdown warrants explanation. Pipeline access required.
  3. Growth Assumptions: The current operating model targets 35% ARR growth for FY2024.
     We view this as optimistic given observed Q4 pipeline dynamics.
  4. Leadership: CTO departure in January 2024 is not yet fully explained. Requires follow-up.

RECOMMENDATION

Advance to Phase 2. Request full CRM export, audited financials FY2021–2023, and updated
management projections. Flag CTO situation for dedicated session with CEO.
