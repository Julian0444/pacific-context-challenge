PARTNER-ONLY · DO NOT DISTRIBUTE — LEGAL DILIGENCE MEMO: pending regulatory inquiry into Meridian revenue recognition practices.
April 16, 2024 | STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL — PARTNER ACCESS ONLY

PREPARED BY: Atlas Legal Diligence Team

SUMMARY

Outside counsel has identified an open inquiry from the Texas Department of Banking related to Meridian's recognition of implementation fees on multi-year ClearFlow contracts. The matter is not public, has not been disclosed in Meridian's 10-K, and should not be distributed outside the partner group until counsel completes the materiality assessment.

The issue centers on whether certain upfront implementation fees were recognized too early for three regional-bank customers signed in late 2022 and early 2023. Management characterizes the inquiry as routine and expects no restatement. Counsel is less definitive: if the regulator concludes that implementation obligations were not substantially complete, Meridian may need to defer $2.8M to $3.6M of previously recognized revenue and revise covenant schedules delivered to lenders.

DEAL IMPLICATION

This does not currently change the recommendation to proceed, but it should be reflected in the purchase agreement through a specific indemnity, a revenue-recognition representation, and a closing condition tied to counsel's final memo. Do not reference this issue in analyst or VP materials until the IC has reviewed the legal recommendation.
